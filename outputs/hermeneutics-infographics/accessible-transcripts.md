# General Hermeneutics Infographic Transcripts

These transcripts are intended to accompany the infographic images on the website. Use the short description as image alt text and place the full transcript immediately after the image or behind a clearly labeled transcript control.

## 1. The Detective Method Overview

Image: `png/01-overview-the-detective-method.png`

Short description: The five phases of General Hermeneutics shown as a detective-style path from first impressions to faithful response.

Full transcript: The Detective Method moves through five phases. Preparation pauses and names assumptions before forming a theory. Observation notices what the passage actually says before explaining it. Interpretation tests the meaning against context, genre, history, grammar, and the whole witness of Scripture. Imagination reconstructs the passage's world without inventing facts. Application carries the interpreted meaning into faithful action. The movement is Prepare, Observe, Interpret, Imagine, Apply. Let the text lead the investigation.

## 2. Preparation

Image: `png/02-preparation.png`

Short description: Preparation begins with prayer, humility, and naming assumptions before studying the passage.

Full transcript: Step 1, Preparation. Enter the Text Humbly. Pray, slow down, and name assumptions before study. Guiding question: What assumptions am I bringing to this passage? Three moves: pray for humility, name your assumptions, and hold conclusions loosely. Look for what you expect the passage to say, old arguments and favorite views, hopes, fears and habits, and resistance to correction. Guardrail: Do not solve the passage before reading it or protect a favorite idea from correction. Remember: The investigator slows down before forming a theory. James 2 clue: Notice the urge to settle the faith-and-works debate immediately. Name that urge, pray, and let the whole passage speak.

## 3. Observation

Image: `png/03-observation.png`

Short description: Observation uses careful reading to record what is present in the biblical text before explaining it.

Full transcript: Step 2, Observation. Notice Before Explaining. Record what the passage actually says before explaining it. Guiding question: What is actually present in the text? Three moves: read and reread, mark the passage, and record unresolved questions. Look for people, place, time and action; repeated words and ideas; commands, questions and promises; and contrasts and connectives. Guardrail: Do not explain too soon or build conclusions on vague impressions. Remember: The investigator works the room before explaining it. James 2 clue: Faith, works, dead and justified repeat. The needy believer, Abraham, Rahab, and the body-without-spirit analogy carry the argument.

## 4. Interpretation

Image: `png/04-interpretation.png`

Short description: Interpretation tests a proposed meaning against context, genre, history, grammar, and Scripture.

Full transcript: Step 3, Interpretation. Test the Meaning. Test the reading against context, genre, history, grammar, and Scripture. Guiding question: What did the author intend to communicate? Three moves: start with local context, test every explanation, and state the meaning clearly. Look for local and whole-book context, genre and literary form, historical and cultural setting, and grammar and key words. Guardrail: Reject readings that ignore context, flatten genre, or escape through cross-references. Remember: The investigator tests explanations against the evidence. James 2 clue: James confronts claimed faith without obedient mercy. Works do not purchase salvation; they reveal that faith is living.

## 5. Imagination

Image: `png/05-imagination.png`

Short description: Imagination carefully reconstructs the passage's world while remaining tethered to textual evidence.

Full transcript: Step 4, Imagination. Rebuild the World Carefully. Enter the passage's world carefully without inventing meaning. Guiding question: What would this have felt like to the first hearers? Three moves: enter the setting, feel the human pressure, and stay tethered to evidence. Look for setting, distance and movement; conflict and social pressure; emotion supplied by the text; and the difference between their world and our world. Guardrail: Use imagination to feel the meaning, never to create facts, doctrine, or meaning. Remember: The investigator reconstructs the scene without inventing facts. James 2 clue: Feel the emptiness of telling a cold, hungry believer to be warmed and filled while offering no help.

## 6. Application

Image: `png/06-application.png`

Short description: Application carries the interpreted meaning into a specific and faithful act of obedience.

Full transcript: Step 5, Application. Respond Faithfully. Carry the interpreted meaning into one concrete act of obedience. Guiding question: How should this meaning reshape life today? Three moves: state the verdict, draw the faithful principle, and choose a concrete action. Look for the meaning for the first audience, an enduring faithful principle, a response rooted in interpretation, and specific measurable obedience. Guardrail: Do not apply before interpreting, stay vague, or turn study into information without obedience. Remember: The investigator writes the report and acts on the findings. James 2 clue: Living faith moves toward need. Identify one concrete need and offer practical help this week.
